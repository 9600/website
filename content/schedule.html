Distribution Miniconf

As the number of requests for developer rooms grew and grew over the years, with having only limited (albeit quite extensive) infrastructure to put at the disposal of open source projects, combined with the fact that a significant fraction of those requests came from distribution projects (Linux and *BSD distributions), we decided to start doing a "conference in the conference" with several goals in mind:

  • avoid having almost all the developer rooms taken by distribution projects
  • give a change for all distribution projects that want to take part into the conference to do so
  • incite collaboration amongst such projects, as while do each do have their specificities, they also do share a lot of tasks, goals, tools and concepts (and, after all, fostering collaboration is the prime goal of FOSDEM)

Hence, in 2011, we started organising a developer room on the topic of distributions, with participants coming from many different such (open source) projects, and we dubbed it "distro miniconf".

As there are many participants and a wealth of interesting topics to talk about, present and share, the <%= l '/distrominiconf/' %> takes place in two rooms across both days of the conference.

Lightning Talks

Lightning Talks are a very popular format that is used at many conferences, where speakers have a mere 15 minutes at their disposal to showcase a Free or Open Source project, an idea, or a concept thereof.

While that brief lapse of time may seem awkward, it almost always leads the presenters to concentrate on the absolute essence and what is really important, which is why it is often a much appreciated approach, along side with the variety of the topics.
